Ship's Crane Professional Operating Guide

Reference outline for hoisting, luffing, slewing, and hydraulic care with a built-in checklist simulator.

Pre-Operation Checklist

    Core Movements

    Hoist raise/lower the load with smooth, metered speed.

    Luff boom in/out; never over-ride limits or sudden changes.

    Slew rotate the crane, prioritize wind, deck traffic, and blind spots.

    Hydraulic & Power Discipline

    • Warm up oil to operating temperature before full-load lifts; monitor pressure gauges and tank levels.
    • Keep hydraulic filters and strainers within service intervals; record delta-P across filters.
    • Inspect hoses for abrasion, leaks, and coupling integrity every watch; tag out any defect immediately.
    • Bleed air after maintenance, verify relief valve settings, and confirm accumulator pre-charge.
    • For electric cranes, check bus voltage, protection relays, insulation resistance, and brake settings.

    Safe Work Practices

    • Only operate within the certified load chart; apply vessel heel/trim and wind corrections.
    • Maintain clear communications with a designated signaller on UHF/VHF or hand signals.
    • Never ride the hook; use certified lifting gear with valid color-coding and SWL markings.
    • Pause operations during heavy weather, poor visibility, or unsecure deck traffic.
    • Test brakes, limit switches, and overload protection each shift.

    Shutdown & Post-Use

    • Lower hook to a safe rest, boom to stow position, slew to the cradle, and engage slew lock.
    • Neutralize controls, de-energize power pack, close isolation valves, and apply lockout/tagout.
    • Log pressures, temperatures, alarms, and any anomalies for maintenance follow-up.
    • Grease slew ring, sheaves, and wire ropes per OEM intervals; wipe down for corrosion control.
